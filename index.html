TERRIFYING LOCAL RULES/CUSTOMS

Because it's no fun unless you're in danger!

June 27

 

Gathered from the best scare stories I can locate

  • There is a strict ban on drinking in public places in Poland and police can impose fines of up to 100zl (about £20); of more concern is that it is technically illegal to be drunk in public and you could end up in the drunk tank. Tied to a bed after being stripped, so be advised not to push your luck with Polish police.

     

    I already regret giving Chet ideas.

     

    •  

  • Jaywalking is a thing here. Cross the road at the wrong spot and the first thing police will ask for is a 'legitimacja' or an official ID. (Always have some form of official ID). You will also be charged 500zl, so find a crossing.

    •  

  • Polish drivers don't stop at zebra crossings. Ever. Don't tempt them. And do remember to look right...Poland has one of the worst road safety records in Europe

    •  

  • Shops shut on Saturdays at 2pm, so get your bottles of water when we land.

    •  

  • Never say 'thank you' when handing over the money after a meal, if you expect change, because the waiter will assume they can keep it.

    •  

  • If you tender anything bigger than a 10zl note in shops you will almost always be asked for something smaller.

    •  

  • Smoking - Banned in pubs which are smaller than 100 square meters or 1 room, but a pack of 20 will cost me £2 so win some lose some. Also if you are going for a smoke with no outdoor tables, leave your pint, otherwise this is drinking outdoors (see point 1).

    •  

  • There is no shortage of public toilets but expect to pay a nominal fee because they are manned by an irritable old lady's, constantly cleaning it.

    •  

  • It's Central Europe you are in, not Eastern Europe, which is composed of the states that followed the break-up of the USSR. Never, ever, use any German or Russian in fun or you'll have your head stoved in.

    •  

  • Buy tram/bus tickets from the larger stations, kiosks, and on the vehicles themselves. And then validate it when on the tram/bus, plain clothes bastards exist to fine tourists who don't get that even though you bought the ticket, it doesn't mean anything until it's validated.

    •  

  • Genuine, regulated taxis will have the rate-per-kilometre displayed in an obvious place (2.80zl - 3.40zl is normal) and an equally obvious meter ticking away as you roll (usually with a minimum fee already displayed when you start). Genuine taxis also look like taxis, with large taxi-company logos and phone numbers emblazoned on them. If you're not sure that the guy toting for fares is a genuine taxi driver, he probably isn't. And even if he is, if he covers the fare meter then expect to be ripped off.

    •  

  • Mike! If two gorgeous ladies stop you on the street and invite you for a drink then it's a scam. Follow them to 'a little bar' they know, buy them a couple of cocktails, and you'll find yourself facing a bill for several hundred zl.

  • Police are known to be armed with, shotguns with riot batton rounds, dogs trained to aim for the pods, standard 9mm's, and a delightful sonic device that will make you wet yourself. But only when the football is on!

     

     

  • Roman Polanski shagged a teenager up the arse. But don't you do it, or you'll get in trouble!

    •  

  • Poland is the most Catholic country in Europe, with 91% of the population being a part of the Catholic Church, which is even more than Ireland. So might want to leave the pentagrams at home.
